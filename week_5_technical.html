Pair Programming

Pair programming has been something I've yet to get totally comfortable with. I think that the issue I've had is I want to take time and think through a problem rather than immediately talk it out. Getting over that has been hard but I've just started telling people when we start on a problem let's both take a few minutes to grasp this on our own and then come back to it. This has worked well because I can speak to the problem much more effectively if I know what the problem is all about.

Getting over some of the nerves and working with other people has been really great and a helpful learning tool. Not only do I feel that I am able to get a different perspective on a problem but I can also bounce ideas off of my partner and get some feedback. What I've enjoyed most about pair programming is the rapid transfer of knowledge. Sometimes when I am working alone I sit on a specific block or bit of code for an hour or so before I can figure out what is wrong. But with a different set of eyes on something that takes mintues and you can learn much more quickly.

Of course once the pairing is over you have to wait for feedback! I've yet to receive feedback I thought was truly negative or it hurt to read. I rarely feel nervous or anxious about feedback. I think a lot of that comes from my athletic background and constantly being coached from other players and coaches on staff. That is something that I feel really helps me in the real world because I feel that I am good at taking criticism and using it to make myself better. Based on my feedback I think I should be more active and less quiet when I am unsure of a problem. Even if I am wrong or not entirely sure of what something is doing I can voice my opinion. That is something I am working on as I tend to only want to speak when I am sure of something, which can take a while when you are new to coding.

I think the hardest part of writing feedback was finding things for people to improve upon. I have yet to work with someone where I feel like they are not doing a great job or really helping me learn.
